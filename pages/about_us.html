Five Fatal Mistakes

Beruff & Company will help you avoid mistakes with regard to market assessment, positioning and value proposition, business model configuration, and capital deployment – mistakes that cost companies and investors millions of dollars.

Mistake #1: Falling in love with your technology

You fall in love with your technology, and extrapolate that customers will love it as much as you do. Thus, you imagine that there is a sizable market for it. The reality is that it is a product no one wants.

Mistake #2: Wanting to satisfy all market segments

You want to satisfy all market segments. Thus, you lack focus, which leads to huge confusion among your staff and your customers, lengthens and complicates product development, and ultimately leads to inferior returns on invested capital.

Mistake #3: Equating a need with a business opportunity

You equate a marketplace need with a business opportunity. However, in health care, especially, a need may not necessarily be an opportunity because of conflicting interests and perverse incentives that drive many participants.

Mistake #4: Becoming enamored of complexity

You become enamored of complexity, because, among other things, it makes you feel smart. However, complexity complicates communications and alignment. It compromises execution and stifles creativity. It creates confusion and leads to a questioning of the true focus. Ultimately, there is an inverse relationship between complexity and satisfaction, value, and quality of execution.

Mistake #5: Success leading to complacency or arrogance

Once you achieve marketplace success, you become complacent or arrogant. As a result, you go backward by:

  1. Abusing your position through higher prices or declining service;
  2. Expanding or diversifying indiscriminately; or,
  3. Making value-destructive acquisitions.

Eduardo Beruff Bio

Eduardo Beruff is the founding CEO of Beruff & Company, a healthcare-focused management consultancy. He is an entrepreneurial leader, business strategist, and advisor with a broad and deep understanding of, and expertise in, the healthcare sector.

Through Beruff & Company, he is committed to sharing the strategies he developed and deployed during his three-decade career to help entrepreneurs, business leaders, and investors realize their companies’ potential. His expertise spans business creation and growth, business and financial strategy formulation, optimizing new product development and positioning, investment evaluation, organizational design and productivity, and enhancing operational performance through improved scalability, margins, and cash flow.

Prior to creating Beruff & Company, Mr. Beruff was the founding CEO of eviti, Inc. Under his leadership, eviti became the Nation’s leading oncology decision-support platform company, connecting providers and payers at the moment of clinical need to facilitate quality care and its payment.

While building eviti, he was a Lecturer and Mentor in Entrepreneurial Leadership and Value Creation at the Wharton Executive MBA program from 2005 through 2009.

He began his career at SmithKline Beckman (now GlaxoSmithKline), where he held a variety of senior-level positions in finance, marketing, general management, and corporate planning. He grew the company’s US-based vaccines business from a virtual start-up to a thriving $400 million Division. While overseas, Mr. Beruff guided SmithKline companies to positions of market leadership and higher profitability. As corporate strategist, he helped to develop business and IT strategy across the enterprise.

Mr. Beruff is a graduate of The Wharton School (MBA) and Georgetown University (magna cum laude).

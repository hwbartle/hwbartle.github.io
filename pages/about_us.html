Five Fatal Mistakes

Beruff & Company helps companies and investors in health care avoid multi-million dollar mistakes with regard to market assessment, positioning and value proposition, business model configuration, and capital deployment.

Mistake #1: Falling in love with your technology

In health care, numerous technology deployments have failed, because they interfere with customer and user workflows. These failed deployments cost millions of dollars and damage company reputations.

Mistake #2: Wanting to satisfy many market segments

A major temptation in health care is the desire to serve multiple market segments with the same service or solution. However, many solutions in health care do not adapt easily beyond the primary target segment. Often, these diversification efforts lead to confusion among company staff and customers, lengthen and complicate product development, and ultimately lead to inferior returns on invested capital.

Mistake #3: Equating a need with a business opportunity

Many companies have lost millions of dollars equating a health care need with a commercial opportunity because of they do not account for conflicting interests and perverse incentives that drive many market participants.

Mistake #4: Becoming enamored of complexity

Often, company growth leads to an inward focus driven by more complex organizational demands and relationships. Customers are no longer the true priority. Consequently, they perceive a reduction in the company’s value proposition, leading to an opening for more focused and nimble competitors.

Mistake #5: Success leading to complacency

Marketplace success can lead to complacency. As a result, companies engage in these costly practices:

  1. Improper pricing or declining service;
  2. Indiscriminant expansion or diversification; or,
  3. Value-destructive acquisitions.

Eduardo Beruff Biography

Eduardo Beruff is the Founder and CEO of Beruff & Company, a health care-focused management consultancy. He is an entrepreneurial leader, business strategist, and advisor with a broad and deep understanding of, and expertise in, the health care sector.

Through Beruff & Company, he is committed to sharing the strategies he developed and deployed during his three-decade career to help entrepreneurs, business leaders, and investors realize their companies’ potential. His expertise spans business creation and growth, business and financial strategy formulation, optimizing new product development and positioning, investment evaluation, organizational design and productivity, and enhancing operational performance through improved scalability, margins and cash flow.

Prior to creating Beruff & Company, Mr. Beruff was a co-Founder and CEO of eviti, Inc. Under his leadership, eviti became the United States’ leading oncology decision-support platform company, connecting providers and payers at the critical moments of clinical need to facilitate quality care and its payment.

While building eviti, he was a lecturer and mentor in Entrepreneurial Leadership and Value Creation at the Wharton Executive MBA program.

Mr. Beruff began his career at SmithKline Beckman (now GlaxoSmithKline), where he held a variety of senior-level positions in finance, marketing, general management, and corporate planning. He grew the company’s US-based vaccines business from a virtual start-up to a thriving $400 million division. While overseas, he guided SmithKline companies to positions of market leadership and higher profitability. As corporate strategist, he helped to develop business and IT strategy across the enterprise.

Mr. Beruff is a graduate of The Wharton School (MBA) and Georgetown University (magna cum laude).
